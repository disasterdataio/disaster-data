    About This Dataset

    This explorer contains FEMA disaster declaration and denial data sourced from the FEMA OpenFEMA API, covering all records from 2000 through 2025.

    The dataset includes 50,068 declaration records and 348 denial records maintained and published by the Federal Emergency Management Agency.

    → FEMA OpenFEMA Data Sets

    Data Fields

    • Declaration Type: DR (Major Disaster), EM (Emergency), FM (Fire Management)
    • Processing Time: Incident Begin Date → Declaration Date for approvals; Request Date → Status Date for denials. Records with negative values excluded.
    • Region: FEMA's 10 administrative regions
    • Denial Rate: Denials ÷ Total Requests (Declarations + Denials) × 100

    Key Findings

    • Overall denial rate: 0.69% — FEMA approves the vast majority of requests
    • Average approval time: 30.5 days
    • Average denial time: 61.3 days — nearly 2× longer
    • Denial processing time has tripled since 2000 (22 days → 84 days in 2025)
    • 2020 spike driven almost entirely by COVID-19 Biological declarations
    • FM (Fire Management) fastest at 1.5 days avg; Biological slowest at 62.6 days avg

    Notes & Limitations

    • Declaration browse shows unique disaster events; county-level designations are collapsed into one row per disaster
    • 2026 data is partial and excluded from all analysis
    • Denial records (348) are far fewer than declarations (50,068) — the overall denial rate is less than 1%
    • State denial rates for small states with few requests should be interpreted cautiously
